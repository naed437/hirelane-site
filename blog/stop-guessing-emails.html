Stop Guessing Email Addresses (They're Bouncing)

Every recruiter has done it: Find a candidate on LinkedIn, guess their email is firstname.lastname@company.com, and hit send.

We tested this approach at scale. The results were brutal.

55%
of guessed email patterns bounced

That means more than half your outreach is going nowhere. Worse, high bounce rates hurt your sender reputation, which means even your good emails start landing in spam.

The Patterns We Tested

Pattern Bounce Rate
firstname.lastname@ 52%
firstnamelastname@ 58%
firstname@ 61%
flastname@ 54%
firstnamel@ 57%

None of these patterns reliably work. Companies use different formats, people change jobs, and email systems catch-all or reject unknown addresses.

Why Bounces Kill Your Outreach

  1. Wasted time — You're crafting emails for addresses that don't exist
  2. Damaged sender reputation — Email providers track bounce rates and mark high-bounce senders as spam
  3. Lower deliverability — Once flagged, even valid emails go to spam
  4. Inaccurate metrics — You can't measure response rates if half your emails never arrived

Where to Find Verified Emails

Instead of guessing, find emails that are actually published:

GitHub profiles — Many developers list their email publicly
Personal websites — About/contact pages often have real emails
Conference talks — Speaker pages usually include contact info
Published papers/articles — Academic and technical authors list emails
Company contact pages — Not individual guesses, actual published addresses

The Math: Quality vs Quantity

Let's compare two approaches:

Approach A: Guess 100 emails

  • 55 bounce
  • 45 delivered
  • 10% response rate = 4.5 responses
  • Sender reputation: damaged

Approach B: Send 50 verified emails

  • 5 bounce (10% for job changes etc)
  • 45 delivered
  • 15% response rate (better targeting) = 6.75 responses
  • Sender reputation: healthy

Half the emails, better results.
